Privacy Policy

Effective June 2026  ·  Injustice Reform Network  ·  EIN 41-4321283

1. Who We Are

The Injustice Reform Network (IRN) is a Virginia 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization, EIN 41-4321283, headquartered in Newport News, Virginia. This Privacy Policy covers injusticereformnetwork.org and all IRN digital tools, including the Community Harm Reporting Tool (CHRT).

2. What We Collect

  • CHRT: Zero-knowledge architecture. Client-side AES-256-GCM encryption means your report is encrypted before it leaves your device. IRN does not possess unencrypted identifying information. A decentralized identifier (DID) links reports without identifying the reporter.
  • Newsletter: Email address only. Your email is not shared with third parties. You may unsubscribe at any time.
  • Contact form: Name and email address, for response purposes only. This information is deleted within 90 days if no ongoing correspondence exists.
  • Website: Standard server logs (IP address, browser type, pages visited). These logs are not linked to individuals and are not sold.

3. What We Don't Do

  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data.
  • We do not use tracking pixels or behavioral advertising.
  • We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or similar surveillance-based analytics platforms.
  • We do not retain CHRT reporter identities — we cannot be compelled to produce what we do not possess.

4. CHRT-Specific Privacy

CHRT is designed with Technical Incapacity as a legal protection. Your report is encrypted before it leaves your device using AES-256-GCM. IRN staff see only anonymized, encrypted data.

In the event of a subpoena or legal order, IRN has nothing to produce regarding reporter identity. This is not a policy decision that can be reversed under pressure — it is an architectural fact.

Your account. Your data. Your choice. IRN will never identify a reporter without their explicit written consent.

5. Third-Party Links

IRN links to external resources including partner organizations, government databases, and advocacy tools. IRN is not responsible for the privacy practices of linked sites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any external site you visit.

6. Children

IRN does not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 13. If you believe a child under 13 has submitted information through an IRN property, please contact us so we may remove it.

7. Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. Significant changes will be noted in The Dispatch, IRN's newsletter. The effective date at the top of this page will always reflect the most recent revision.

8. Contact

Questions about this Privacy Policy? Use the contact form. IRN will respond within 3–5 business days.
