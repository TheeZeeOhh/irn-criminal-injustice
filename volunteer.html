Open Roles

Four Ways to Contribute

Community Researcher

File and track FOIA requests, parse public records, and build evidentiary patterns for IRN campaigns and DOJ submissions.

  • FOIA filing
  • Public records
  • Pattern documentation

Know Your Rights Facilitator

Deliver Know Your Rights workshops in Hampton Roads neighborhoods. Written at an 8th-grade reading level. No legal jargon. Training provided.

  • Public speaking
  • Community engagement
  • Hampton Roads based

Case Intake Advocate

Serve as the trauma-informed first point of contact for CHRT reporters. Apply active listening, document accurately, and escalate per CJRRP protocol.

  • Trauma-informed care
  • Case documentation
  • Active listening

Tech & Design Contributor

Build and maintain IRN's digital infrastructure — including the CHRT portal, public-facing website, and data visualization tools.

  • Web development
  • UI/UX design
  • Data visualization
What to Expect

Real Work. Real Support.

IRN volunteers are embedded in real documentation and advocacy work from day one. We provide training, clear protocols, and a community of peers doing the same work.

Training Provided

Role-specific onboarding for every volunteer. No prior experience required for most roles.

Flexible Commitment

We ask for consistency over quantity. Even 4 hours a month moves the work.

Local-First

Volunteers serve Hampton Roads, Virginia and the Baltimore, Maryland region.

Privacy-Protective

IRN's case data protocols protect both volunteers and the communities we serve.
