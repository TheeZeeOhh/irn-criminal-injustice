Case Study · Hampton Roads, Virginia · June 2026

What Accountability Looks Like

In June 2026, Circuit Court Judge Rebecca Robinson dismissed all eight criminal charges against Dr. Ebony Parker, ruling that no criminal act had occurred. IRN documented this case from intake through vindication — proof that disciplined, evidence-grounded advocacy works.

June 2, 2026

Intake

Incident report submitted securely via CHRT portal.

June 3, 2026

Documentation

IRN investigators collect evidence, FOIA records, and witness statements.

June 5, 2026

Attorney Referral

Case matched with a specialized civil rights attorney from our network.

June 10, 2026

Solidarity Statement

Public mobilization and narrative defense campaign launched.

June 24, 2026

Vindication

All charges dismissed. Full accountability achieved.

How It Works

From Report to Legislation

STEP 01

YOU REPORT ANONYMOUSLY

No name. No address. A decentralized identifier (DID) links your reports without identifying you. Client-side AES-256-GCM encryption means IRN cannot be compelled to produce what it does not possess.

STEP 02

IRN DOCUMENTS AND ACTS

Your report enters IRN's civil rights database. If it triggers the CJRRP, an attorney referral and solidarity statement follow within 72 hours.

STEP 03

DATA BECOMES LEGISLATION

Documented patterns become testimony, FOIA requests, DOJ complaints, and Virginia General Assembly evidence through IRN's JFV legislative partnership.
