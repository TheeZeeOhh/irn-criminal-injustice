RECORDS REQUESTED:
[DESCRIBE THE SPECIFIC RECORDS YOU ARE REQUESTING]

I request that you respond to this request within 5 business days, as required by law. If any portion of this request is denied, I request that you:

  1. Identify each document or record withheld;
  2. State the specific statutory exemption claimed for each withheld record; and
  3. Explain why the exemption applies to the withheld material.

If you determine that some portions of a responsive document are exempt from disclosure, I request that all non-exempt portions be released.

If you need clarification regarding this request, please contact me promptly so that we may expedite the response. Please acknowledge receipt of this request in writing.

Your FOIA Rights

Virginia FOIA

All public records are presumed open under Va. Code § 2.2-3700. Agencies bear the burden of proving an exemption applies. You can request records in any medium the agency has them in.

Response: 5 business days · Appeal: circuit court

Maryland PIA

Broad right of access under Md. Code Ann., GP § 4-101. Custodians must grant or deny access in writing. You can appeal to the Maryland Public Access Ombudsman at no cost before going to circuit court.

Response: 10 business days · Appeal: Circuit Court

Common Exemptions

Agencies may withhold ongoing investigation records, personal privacy information, attorney-client privileged material, and personnel files. Exemptions are narrow — partial release is required when only portions qualify.

Demand the specific code section in writing

If Denied

Request a written explanation citing the exact statutory exemption. Virginia: appeal to the Virginia FOIA Council (free) or circuit court. Maryland: contact the Public Access Ombudsman or file in circuit court.
