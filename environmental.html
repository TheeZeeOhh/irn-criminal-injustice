Our Programs

Six Ways We Fight Environmental Injustice

Air Quality Monitoring

Community-deployed sensors in Environmental Justice zones track particulate matter, ozone, and toxin levels. Data is logged, timestamped, and submitted to state regulators.

Lead Exposure Documentation

IRN partners with residents to document lead paint, lead pipes, and contaminated soil — especially in pre-1978 housing stock concentrated in majority-Black neighborhoods.

Industrial Polluter Accountability

We track permit violations, unpermitted discharges, and odor complaint patterns near industrial facilities in low-income zip codes and push for regulatory enforcement.

FOIA Requests on EPA & State Violations

IRN has filed 12 FOIA requests targeting suppressed inspection records, delayed enforcement actions, and internal communications on Environmental Justice communities.

Community Health Surveys

Structured health surveys administered door-to-door capture asthma rates, skin conditions, neurological symptoms, and self-reported exposures for aggregate analysis.

Legislative Advocacy

IRN testifies before the Virginia General Assembly, submits public comment on EPA rulemaking, and co-sponsors environmental justice bills with allied organizations.

Case Study · Hampton Roads, Virginia · Ongoing

PFAS Contamination Near Norfolk Naval Station

Communities adjacent to military installations in Hampton Roads face elevated PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substance) contamination from decades of firefighting foam use. These "forever chemicals" are linked to cancer, thyroid disease, and immune dysfunction — and they concentrate in drinking water, soil, and fish tissue.

IRN is documenting the geographic pattern of PFAS exposure in majority-Black neighborhoods near Naval Station Norfolk, NAS Oceana, and Langley Air Force Base. Our field teams collect water samples, cross-reference DoD inspection records obtained via FOIA, and map contamination plumes against census tract demographics.

The data is being compiled into an evidentiary package for submission to the Virginia Department of Environmental Quality and the EPA's PFAS National Enforcement and Compliance Initiative.
