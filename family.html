Our Programs

Six Ways We Defend Families

CPS Case Accompaniment

Trauma-informed advocates accompany parents through every stage of a CPS case — initial contact, home visits, family team meetings, and court hearings — ensuring rights are known and exercised.

Manifestation Determination Review Support

IRN reviews IEPs, disciplinary records, and procedural histories before MDR meetings, and sends trained advocates to stand beside parents. 68% of MDR cases reviewed by IRN found procedural violations.

Reproductive Health Access Advocacy

IRN documents reproductive health inequities — maternal mortality disparities, forced sterilization history, and barriers to contraceptive care — and advocates for structural remedies.

Family Reunification Support

IRN has supported 23 families through reunification, navigating service plan compliance, court requirements, and housing instability that courts weaponize against parents.

Know Your Rights for Parents

Free workshops covering CPS entry rights, the right to refuse consent to a home search, what constitutes mandatory reporting, and how to document interactions for your own protection.

Trans Family Support (TGEPOC Focus)

IRN prioritizes Trans, Gender-Expansive, and People of Color families facing compounded surveillance from CPS, schools, and healthcare systems. Affirming, confidential accompaniment available.

Systemic Pattern · Hampton Roads Schools · Ongoing

The School-to-Family-Separation Pipeline

In Hampton Roads schools, routine disciplinary referrals — in-school suspensions, behavioral incident reports, calls to school resource officers — are routinely escalating into CPS contact. IRN has documented a pattern in which a single school discipline record triggers a mandatory reporter call, which triggers a CPS investigation, which triggers a family team meeting, which becomes grounds for a safety plan — all without a single finding of abuse or neglect.

Black students represent a disproportionate share of these referrals. The pipeline is not a bug; it is a feature of systems designed to surveil families that have been systematically denied economic stability, adequate housing, and mental health resources.

IRN is building a case-by-case database of school discipline referrals that preceded CPS contact, cross-referenced against student disability status, race, and school district. This dataset will anchor legislative testimony and potential civil rights complaints to the U.S. Department of Education Office for Civil Rights.
