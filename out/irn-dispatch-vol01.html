Community Education · Post-Conviction Series

Protect Your Investment: The Anti-Fraud Workshop Series Continues

Families navigating post-conviction relief are targets — for grief-priced fees, phantom advocates, and contracts written to confuse. IRN's workshop series exists to change that math, with sessions serving families across Hampton Roads and Baltimore. The network teaches what nobody else will: how to vet before you pay.

The standing rules of the series:

  • Do your homework. A lot of folks are frauds. Verify before a dollar moves.
  • Read the contract. All of it. Every time.
  • Pay with certified funds and document the memo: "Post Conviction Relief," the advocate's name, organization, and remaining balance.
  • Understand what an advocate can and can't do — legally and practically.
  • What it look like don't mean it is what it is. Verify. Verify. Verify.
  • Protect your investment — and as a gift from the network, we teach you how.

Next session dates announced via the IRN Facebook page and Member Platform notifications.

Signal Boost · The Reading Room

From Inside the System to Against It

Recommended reading from the network this month: David A. Love's profile in ContrabandCamp of North Carolina organizer Amanda Wallace — a former Child Protective Services insider who founded Operation Stop CPS after witnessing the system's abuses firsthand and concluding that the family policing apparatus can't be reformed from within.

Why it matters here: family separation and the carceral system are the same pipeline wearing different uniforms. Wallace's insider-to-abolitionist arc echoes the framework IRN organizes from — the people closest to the harm are closest to the solution, and sometimes the most credible witness is the one who used to hold the clipboard.
